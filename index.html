Why is the Croissant so popular? 🥐

Croissants are immensely popular due to their unique, luxurious texture—characterized by a crisp, flaky exterior and a light, airy, buttery interior. Their appeal stems from a satisfying, rich flavor, versatile suitability for both sweet and savory applications, and their iconic status as a symbol of French culinary elegance.

The History of the Croissant

Contrary to popular belief, the croissant has its origins in Austria, not France. It was inspired by the Austrian Kipferl.
